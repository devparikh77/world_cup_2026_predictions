V2: After Round 1

This tab is reserved for the next forecast version. Once every team has played its first group match, V2 will replace those simulated Round 1 results with actual outcomes, update group-local states, and rerun the remaining tournament tree.

V3: After Rounds 1-2

This tab is reserved for the forecast after each team has played twice. V3 will use actual Round 1 and Round 2 outcomes to update standings, third-place qualification, and the projected knockout paths.

Methodology

1. Start With Priors

The model starts with recency-weighted international Elo, tournament weights, host advantage, confederation adjustments, capped Transfermarkt squad market-value adjustments, and available betting priors.

2. Sample Scorelines

Each match uses a low-score-adjusted Poisson scoreline model. This produces a full distribution over scores, not just win/draw/loss probabilities.

3. Update State Sequentially

After each sampled scoreline, the model updates team ratings and group standings before the next match in that simulated tournament.

4. Build The Bracket

After group play, the model ranks groups, assigns top-two and best third-place qualifiers, then simulates knockout matches round by round.

5. Repeat Many Times

Each simulation produces one possible tournament. Stage and champion probabilities are simply the share of simulations where each team reaches that stage.

6. Version Causally

V1 uses no actual World Cup results. V2 will use actual Round 1 results only after all Round 1 games are complete. V3 will use actual Rounds 1 and 2.

Worked Example: Mexico vs South Africa

Base Elo Mexico 1784.0 · South Africa 1613.7

Ratings come from completed international matches before the tournament cutoff, with recent competitive matches weighted more heavily.

Context Priors Host +70 · Confed -10/-2

Mexico receives host advantage. Both teams also receive modest confederation adjustments.

Market Inputs Squad adj -0.4/-18.0

Transfermarkt squad market values are capped so they act as a small prior, not the main model driver.

Final Ratings Mexico 1843.7 · South Africa 1593.7

Base Elo plus context, market value, and betting priors produce the ratings used for the match probability.

Attack/Defense Form Attack z -0.61/-0.29

Recent scoring and defending residuals adjust expected goals relative to rating-based expectation.

Match Probability 71.7% / 20.1% / 8.1%

Expected goals are 2.0-0.5. The simulation samples scorelines from this distribution.

In the Monte Carlo tree, a sampled Mexico-South Africa score updates both teams' ratings before their next simulated group match. Across 1,000 simulations, this creates a distribution of tournament paths rather than one fixed bracket.
